Currency Pairs Explained

⏱ 8 min read 📊 Beginner ✓ Lesson 3 of 5

What is a Currency Pair?

In forex trading, currencies are always quoted in pairs. When you trade EUR/USD, you're simultaneously buying the Euro and selling the US Dollar. The price tells you how much of the quote currency you need to buy one unit of the base currency.

For example, if EUR/USD = 1.1532, it means 1 Euro buys 1.1532 US Dollars.

💡 Key Insight

Every forex trade involves two currencies — which is why understanding the relative strength between two economies is the foundation of fundamental analysis.

Base Currency
The first currency in a pair. In EUR/USD, EUR is the base.
Quote Currency
The second currency. In EUR/USD, USD is the quote.
Pip
Smallest standard price move. For most pairs = 0.0001.
Spread
Difference between bid and ask. The broker's fee.
